Landing a PhD position and graduating with a PhD are two different things but I think I can comment on the first matter and keep my comments updated with feedback.

Following is my Statement of Purpose (SOP) for my PhD application at UCSD.

SOP_UCSD.pdf

It is not the greatest SOP ever. For instancce, I advise that you should definitely state the professors of whose research is in your interest in your SOP.

Be humble about your success and do not hide your failures. Instead, focus how you overcame your "failures" by doing or achieving "what" afterwards. (A faculty member told me about this after I started my PhD and reading my SOP)

You might find it useful to include hyperlinks and bold texts whenever necessary to make your point. You possibly have very good qualities and experience, focus on them and use a winner tone.

You should by default believe that "you'd be successful and sleeping on your application would be their lost". This doesn't necessarily mean thinking highly of yourself. If you're applying for a PhD, seeking references letters to support it, you should be serious about this process. Reflect this in your SOP. Think of it as a matching process between you and the department on your and their estimation of yourself (past achievements, social skills, hard skills, etc) and potential.

Follow the other usual advices, start early (sometimes even writing a SOP takes days/weeks), do good, etc.

You are more than welcome to send an email to me! Good Luck!
